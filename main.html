Alphabet

This language makes use of both the Asomtavruli and the Nuskhuri scripts. While the Asomtavruli script is used to represent the language as abjad, the Nuskhuri script is used to represent the language with a more traditional alphabet. When writing in Nuskhuri, while using Asomtavruli for uppercase letters, this system is know as Khutsuri.

One of the characteristics this language posesses is the mutation of consonants when facing palatalization. The rule is how the labializated consonants lose labialization, while nonlabialized consonants overpalatalize if it had a labialized counterpart.

Voiced consonants devoice at the end of a word, or when followed by a voiceless consonant. By the other hand, a voiceless consonant becomes voiced when followed by a voiced consonant. When the voiced consonant devoices at the end of a word, it also loses its labialization.

Words

This language makes use of very marked use of ablaut, which is a feature of the language that involves the alternation of vowels in related words, while keeping in place the consonant roots of the words.

Verbs

Verbs can be divided into 2 categories:

  • Perfective, which always have 3 consonant roots.
  • Imperfective, which always have 4 consonant roots.
